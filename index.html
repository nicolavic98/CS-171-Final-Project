Getting Educated:
An Analysis of Post-Secondary Education

Higher education has been called into question for a number of reasons recently. We examine how educational achievement has changed over the years and if the United States is accommodating the needs of the education-seeking population.

Who's going to college?

We begin our analysis by providing an overview of regional changes in the number of students going to college.


The data supports claims that the number of students pursuing college degrees has increased. We can see that there has been a fairly consistent increase in the number of students going to college over the past almost-five decades.

It's critical to note that states with much larger populations than others, by default, will send more students to college than others just based on population.

Has affordability of college changed?

A huge factor we often don't see accurate data for is financial aid.
With college tuition soaring over the decades, how has financial aid stacked up?

Here, we can see that the aid a student receives on average has stayed about the same. Because it has not grown to match the tuition, there is a bigger gap between what a student must pay and how much help they have for that goal. As such, students are put in greater debt than ever.

In Summary

As we can see from the above data, more and more students are attending college. Although more states are sending greater numbers of students to university, the majority of enrolled students are still white. However, especially in recent years, diversity has started increasing more sharply, so we may see the trend change in the future. Those students that are in college are studying STEM subjects more than before, but other fields continue to take the lead, particularly business and other pre-professional majors. Finally, as we can see the cost of college has skyrocketed, while financial aid has stayed rather stagnant over the decades. Perhaps, the United States is in fact not properly accommodating the needs of the education-seeking population.
